A positive mindset brings positive things

The Misurata Free Zone is an area liberated from all tax, customs, commercial, monetary and other restrictions, with the exception of those related to faith, national and national security, health and environmental protection The Misurata Free Zone

is the first free economic zone established on the Libyan territory in accordance with Law No. (9) of 2000 AD regarding free zones and transit trade, with the aim of diversifying sources of income, raising the level of performance of the local economy and opening job and training opportunities, by creating an attractive investment environment for activities The diversified economy and transit trade through optimal utilization of the available local capabilities and resources, on top of which is the distinguished geographical location and the sea port. https://mfzly.com/

Why The Confrence

Hydrocarbon dependency

The dependence of the Libyan economy on hydrocarbon resources is in the range of 70% of GDP, more than 95% of exports, and 90% or more of government revenues.

Opportunities

The existence of inter-investment and trade opportunities across Libya and the continents of the world.

Investment

The free zones and major Mediterranean and African companies are looking forward to investment and trade exchange with the Misurata Free Zone.

Communication

The low quality of the infrastructure for the activity of the free zones represented in logistics and communication technology.

Natural resources

Non-optimal exploitation of the geographical location and natural resources for transit trade and services.

Goals

Improving infrastructure

  • Increasing the performance of infrastructure and the quality of services for free zones

Attracting Investment

  • Attracting foreign and local investment directed to the development of free zones.

Participation

  • Participation of state institutions in activating the legislation and laws regulating free zones.

Strengthening partnerships

  • Strengthening partnerships and international trade between free zones.
